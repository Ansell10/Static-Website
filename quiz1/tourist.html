Purwodadi

Purwodadi is a town in Central Java, Indonesia, known for its cultural and natural attractions

Bledug Kuwu

Bledug Kuwu is a natural attraction in Central Java, Indonesia, known for its periodic eruptions of mineral-rich mud.

Api Abadi Mrapen

Eternal Flame of Mrapen, is a natural gas flame in Central Java, Indonesia. It has been burning continuously for decades and is considered a unique and enduring natural phenomenon.

Waduk Kedungombo

Waduk Kedungombo is a reservoir located in Central Java, Indonesia. It serves various purposes, including irrigation, flood control, and as a source of freshwater supply.

Several Tourist Attractions around Purwodadi

Bledug Kuwu

Mud Vulcano

  • 28 km east of Purwodadi
  • Forms from gas release, mainly methane
  • Periodically releases mineral mud
  • Locals use the mud to make traditional bleng salt

Api Abadi Mrapen

Eternal Flame

  • 26 kilometers east of Purwodadi
  • Leaking of natural gas from the ground
  • Considered sacred in Javanese culture
  • The flame went out on September 25, 2020

Waduk Kedung Ombo

Reservoir

  • 29 kilometers south of Purwodadi
  • Construction began in 1984
  • Irrigate agricultural land covering 60,000 hectares
  • It controls flooding and supplies clean water
